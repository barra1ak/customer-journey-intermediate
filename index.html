The Big Idea

The sale is one moment in a much larger relationship.

Agricultural customers often begin planning months before they purchase. Their experience continues through delivery, changing field conditions, technical support, performance evaluation and preparation for the following season.

Final Activity

Put the agricultural customer journey in order.

Enter a number from 1 through 7 next to each event. Each number should be used once.

Think about how a typical agricultural relationship develops over the course of a season. Planning begins before the order, while support and evaluation continue long afterward.

Post-season outcome review

The customer reflects on results, service and unresolved issues.

Initial planning conversation

The customer identifies goals, risks, needs and possible changes.

In-season support request

Field conditions change and the customer needs timely help.

Comparison of products and programs

The customer evaluates fit, price, support, timing and risk.

Recommendation and customer decision

The customer decides whether the recommendation is credible, valuable and appropriate.

Order and delivery coordination

The customer needs confirmation, visibility and clear next steps.

Renewal recommendation

The organization uses the year’s experience to improve the next plan.

Key Takeaways

The customer journey follows the agricultural year.

Carry these ideas into the final Customer Journey Lab.

The Journey Begins Early

Customer experience starts during planning, problem recognition and initial research—not when the order form appears.

Support Shapes Trust

Delivery visibility, changing field conditions and responsive in-season support often matter as much as the original product recommendation.

Renewal Is Earned

A thoughtful post-season review connects outcomes, friction and relationship memory to the following year’s plan.
